Community Empowerment & Sustainable Agriculture

Alifa Foundation is a non-governmental organization based in Bogor. Established in 2007, it focuses on community empowerment and local sustainability.

We collaborate with various stakeholders to conduct research, conservation, and documentation of local food resources. We also provide guidance and advocacy for family farmers and organic-agroecological producers—including women, youth, indigenous peoples, and local communities—especially in supporting the Participatory Guarantee System (PGS) certification process.

Our mission is to encourage communities to live healthily, organically, and harmoniously with nature in a holistic way, while also providing strategic insights for the development of the organic agriculture sector to stakeholders.

Alifa Foundation's Vision & Mission

As a non-governmental organization based in Bogor since 2007, Alifa Foundation aims to create empowered communities that are healthy and live in harmony with nature. We collaborate with various stakeholders to develop sustainable and equitable local food systems.

Vision

To become a pioneering organization in empowering local communities through organic farming, healthy living, and holistic, equitable ecosystem sustainability.

Mission

  • Provide education and positive influence for a healthy lifestyle that is in harmony with nature.
  • Document, research, and conserve local food resources.
  • Offer support and advocacy to family farmers and agroecological producers.
  • Strengthen the capacity of vulnerable groups, such as women, youth, and indigenous communities.
  • Encourage the implementation and recognition of the Participatory Guarantee System (PGS) certification.
  • Provide strategic advice and recommendations for the development of the organic agriculture sector.

Alifa Foundation's Local & International Activities

Alifa Foundation actively participates in various local and international activities to support organic food sustainability and expand networks within the agricultural sector. From local programs focusing on community empowerment to participation in global events that bring together key stakeholders in agriculture, we continue to innovate and share knowledge.

Indigenous Community Organic Food Roadmap

Alifa Foundation is involved in the Organic Food Roadmap project engaging indigenous communities in seven regions from Java to Papua (2021-2023), to promote organic farming based on local wisdom.

Participatory Guarantee System (PGS)

We support the PGS system for seven organic commodities: Tengkawang, Palm Sugar, Honey, Salt, Organic Rice, Organic Arabica Coffee, and Sago, ensuring environmentally friendly and accountable organic products.

Organic World Congress

Our participation in the Organic World Congress (November 2024 - December 2024) in Taiwan strengthens Alifa Foundation's commitment to the development of organic agriculture and global food sustainability.

Terra Madre Salone del Gusto - Italy

Alifa Foundation participates in the Terra Madre Salone del Gusto event in Turin, Italy, held annually as an international forum to discuss organic agriculture and food sustainability (2012, 2014, 2016, 2018).

Global Events 2023

In 2023, Alifa Foundation participated in several international events across the globe, including in Rome, South Korea, the Philippines, Vietnam, and Dubai, to deepen insights into the latest trends in organic farming and sustainability.

COP 28 & INOFO

Alifa Foundation participated in the United Nations Climate Change Conference (COP 28) at the end of 2023 and will take part in INOFO in Tanzania (2025) to advocate for sustainable solutions in organic agriculture.
